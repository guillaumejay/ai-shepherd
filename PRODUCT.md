# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AI Shepherd is for solo developers who use local AI coding tools and terminal workflows, especially Claude Code, WezTerm, PowerShell, and WSL on Windows. They want a quiet companion that shows what is happening without interrupting their work.

Users are usually mid-flow: coding, reviewing terminal output, watching AI usage, or switching between sessions. They need quick confidence, not a second workspace to manage.

## Product Purpose

AI Shepherd is a local desktop utility for monitoring AI coding tool usage and terminal sessions. It exists to make opaque AI sessions more visible, more accountable, and easier to track while keeping captured data on the user's machine.

Success means the user can glance at the tray app and understand current usage, active sessions, and terminal context without reading logs or leaving their development loop.

## Brand Personality

Calm, competent, watchful.

The product should feel like a steady local monitor: technical enough to be trusted by developers, warm enough to avoid enterprise coldness, and restrained enough to stay out of the way. The shepherd metaphor can inform tone and visual warmth, but it should not turn the interface into a mascot product.

## Anti-references

- Crypto-terminal styling: neon-on-black, hacker theatrics, aggressive contrast, synthetic glow.
- Enterprise admin dashboards: cold CRUD tables, generic panels, dense chrome, management-console language.
- AI SaaS clichés: purple gradients, glass cards, hype copy, generic hero metrics.
- Cute mascot apps: sheep jokes, pastoral illustration overload, playful copy that weakens trust.
- Cloud-first positioning: anything that implies remote capture, team surveillance, or vendor lock-in.

## Design Principles

1. Local trust first: make privacy and on-device behavior feel inherent, not bolted on.
2. Glanceable before comprehensive: the first read should answer what is active, what changed, and whether attention is needed.
3. Technical without ceremony: expose useful details, but avoid management-dashboard clutter.
4. Quiet warmth: borrow subtle pastoral cues from the icon through tone and color, not decoration.
5. Respect flow: the app should support coding momentum, never compete with the editor or terminal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, and legible type. Support reduced motion where animations are introduced. Avoid relying on color alone for status. Design for extended desktop use, including small utility windows and lower-light working conditions.
